# EVP Content Inventory

## Core EVP Statement

Arabian Drilling offers careers for people who want to work safely, grow through experience, and contribute to reliable energy operations across Saudi Arabia. In return, AD expects commitment to safety, teamwork, technical excellence, integrity, and reliable performance.

## EVP Pillars

| Pillar | Candidate promise | Employee expectation |
|---|---|---|
| Safety First | Work in an environment where safe behavior, risk awareness, and care for others matter. | Take responsibility for your own safety and the safety of your team. |
| Growth Through Experience | Learn through training, mentoring, feedback, and practical operational exposure. | Stay curious, keep improving, and take ownership of development. |
| Technical Excellence | Work with experienced teams, critical assets, and high operational standards. | Bring professionalism, discipline, accuracy, and attention to detail. |
| Purpose and National Contribution | Contribute to reliable energy operations and local capability in Saudi Arabia. | Act with pride, responsibility, and long-term thinking. |
| Teamwork and Human Connection | Work with people who rely on each other in demanding environments. | Communicate clearly, support colleagues, and respect different backgrounds. |
| Wellbeing and Resilience | Join a culture that values readiness, health, care, and sustainable performance. | Work responsibly, follow procedures, and maintain readiness for the role. |

## What AD Offers

- Safety-first work culture and clear expectations around responsible performance.
- Hands-on learning through real operational challenges.
- Exposure to onshore, offshore, HSE, maintenance, engineering, supply chain, corporate, and early-career paths.
- Work connected to Saudi Arabia’s energy sector and local talent development.
- Team-based environments where communication, trust, and reliability matter.

## What AD Expects

- Commitment to safety, teamwork, integrity, and technical discipline.
- Care for people, assets, customers, communities, and the environment.
- Clear communication, practical problem solving, and reliable follow-through.
- Ownership of development and readiness for role-specific requirements.

## Editorial Guardrails

- Do not invent compensation, benefits, program names, eligibility, awards, or internal policies.
- Use neutral language where proof is not confirmed: “Benefits may vary by role and location,” “Program details should be confirmed by Talent Acquisition,” and “Career paths vary by function, experience, and business needs.”
- Keep candidate-facing content practical, role-relevant, and connected to AD’s safety-first operating environment.
