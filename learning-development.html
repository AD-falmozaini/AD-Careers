Growth through experience

Capability develops closest to the work

AD career growth should be understood as a mix of structured learning, supervisor feedback, safety discipline, and practical exposure. Program details should be confirmed by Talent Acquisition.

Learning through experience

Operational work builds judgment, confidence, and readiness through real tasks, site context, and practical problem solving.

Technical training

Role-based development may support drilling, maintenance, HSE, logistics, digital, and corporate capability needs.

Safety training

Risk awareness, procedure discipline, and safe behaviors are part of development across functions.

Leadership development

Progression can include communication, supervision, decision-making, and coaching capability.

Mentoring and feedback

Experienced colleagues, managers, and project teams help employees understand expectations and improve.

Career progression clarity

Career paths vary by function, experience, performance, and business needs, so candidates should review each posting carefully.

What AD expects

Own your development

Employees are expected to stay curious, learn from experience, keep safety and quality at the center of their work, and build the capabilities their role requires.

  • For studentsBuild foundations through internships, graduate pathways, and entry-level roles.
  • For professionalsDeepen technical, operational, leadership, and cross-functional capability.
  • For leadersCoach teams, communicate clearly, and make safety-minded decisions.
