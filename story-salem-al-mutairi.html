Questions and answers

Salem reflects on operational leadership.

What changed when you became a supervisor?

I became responsible for the whole crew, the plan, the risks, and whether everyone understood the standard.

How do you build trust?

I try to be consistent. People need to know that safety concerns will be heard.

What is the hardest part?

The pressure can be high, but the standard cannot move.

How do you coach different experience levels?

I adjust the conversation to the person, but I keep the standard clear for everyone.

What does speaking up mean to you?

It means people trust that concerns will be taken seriously before they become incidents.

What advice would you give future leaders?

Master the basics first. Be visible, listen carefully, and lead through daily behavior.
